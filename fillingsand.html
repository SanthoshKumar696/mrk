Filling Sand

Filling sand, also known as backfill sand, is a type of sand used primarily for filling up spaces or voids in construction projects. It's commonly used in various stages of construction, landscaping, and for creating a solid base for laying foundations, paving, or landscaping. Filling sand typically has larger grains compared to other types of sand like river or masonry sand, making it more suitable for use as a filler.

Gravel Sand

Gravel is a loose aggregation of rock fragments which is formed as a result of sedimentary and erosive geologic processes. It is commonly produced in large quantities for commercial purposes. Gravel can be classified by its grain size range with the Udden-Wentworth scale which categorizes particles by size.

  • Coarse Texture: Enhances cement adhesion.
  • High Strength: Suitable for robust construction.
  • Durable: Withstands high loads.

Red soil sand

Red soil is a type of soil that is rich in iron oxide, giving it a reddish color. It's found in warm, humid climates and is often used for agriculture.

  • Climate:Develops in warm, temperate, and humid climates.
  • Fertility: High levels of organic matter and nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.
  • Drainage: Well-drained, which is important for agriculture.
