Jalli

Jali is a traditional architectural feature commonly found in South Asian, Islamic, and Mughal architecture, made from materials such as stone, wood, brick, concrete, stainless steel, or cast iron. These intricately carved screens feature geometric, floral, or arabesque patterns, allowing for the filtration of light, ventilation, and privacy. They create a soft, diffused light effect while providing natural airflow to cool spaces, making them ideal for hot climates. Beyond their functionality, jalis also enhance the aesthetic beauty of buildings, serving as a decorative element in courtyards, windows, and doorways. Traditionally crafted by skilled artisans, they reflect cultural heritage and architectural mastery, and their use continues in modern architecture for both decorative and sustainable purposes.

Available Sizes

40mm 20mm 12mm 6mm

Jalli - 40mm

40mm jalli is a coarse aggregate primarily used in heavy construction and foundation work requiring high structural strength.

  • Foundation work: Ideal for concrete foundations and footings.
  • Heavy construction: Used in dams, bridges, and retaining walls.
  • Drainage: Excellent for drainage applications and road base layers.

Jalli - 20mm

20mm jalli is the most commonly used size in construction, providing an optimal balance of strength and workability.

  • Standard concrete: Ideal for columns, beams, and slabs.
  • RCC work: Perfect for reinforced cement concrete applications.
  • General construction: Used in residential and commercial buildings.

Jalli - 12mm

12mm jalli is used for more refined concrete applications requiring better finish and moderate strength.

  • Thin structures: Suitable for thinner structural elements.
  • Decorative concrete: Used in architectural elements and finishes.
  • Flooring: Provides good finish for concrete flooring applications.

Jalli - 6mm

6mm jalli is the finest aggregate size, primarily used for applications requiring smooth finishes and detailed work.

  • Fine finishes: Perfect for smooth concrete surfaces and detailed work.
  • Plastering: Used in plastering and repair applications.
  • Decorative elements: Ideal for terrazzo flooring and architectural details.
